Frequently Asked Questions

We’ve gathered answers to common questions families ask when exploring home support services. If you don’t see your question here, call us anytime — we’re happy to help.

1. What areas in Sudbury do you serve?

We provide in‑home support throughout Greater Sudbury and nearby communities. If you’re unsure whether we cover your area, give us a quick call.

2. Are your caregivers medically trained?

All personal support workers (PSWs) and caregivers are certified, background‑checked, and supervised by our physician‑led care team for safety and quality assurance.

3. What makes We Care Sudbury different from other home care providers?

We’re doctor‑led — meaning every plan benefits from medical insight, not just home assistance. We offer personalized coordination, family updates, and genuine compassion.

4. Can you help with medications or wellness checks?

Yes. Our caregivers provide reminders, monitor daily wellbeing, and communicate any concerns promptly to families and healthcare professionals.

5. Is there a minimum number of hours or visits per week?

No. We’re flexible — from occasional wellness check‑ins to daily support. We design schedules that match family needs and budgets.

6. How do you match caregivers with families?

We meet with each family to understand preferences, needs, and personality fit, then assign caregivers who share the family’s values and communication style.

7. How do families receive updates?

You’ll receive ongoing communication by phone, email, or dashboard summaries so you always know how your loved one is doing.

8. How can we get started?

Simple — book a free consultation online or call our care coordination team. We’ll create a personalized plan and start support as soon as you’re ready.
